I am, at best, an adequate amateur general scientist. I work professionally in a scientific field, but it is not this field. The data and commentary on which I rely is not produced by me, but by experts. If any of what I write is not in keeping with the best reading of the data, or if I have misrepresented the expert commentary I cite, please correct me. You can issue a Pull Request here.

Let me wear my politics on my sleeve for a moment: my efforts in research and advocacy have nothing to do with saving something called "the economy." Although I'm generally an optimist, I think that these days of the virus present a reminder of the need to consider our communities, our freedom, our future.

In fact, if the economies of imperialism and environmental destruction are disrupted and irreversibly hamstrung by our response to this pandemic, I view that as a pleasant bonus effect. My only concern is saving lives and quality of life. If economic recovery can do that, I will grudgingly support it, although I hope that we also take this opportunity to grow out of the childish economic configuration that has characterized the United States; that it has been so easily disrupted is an indicator of its unsustainability in the first place.

None of what I write is meant to be defense of the White House. I supported the impeachment of the 45th President, and I will gladly support it again, whether on the basis of the astonishingly blunderous response to this pandemic or any number of other crimes, with removal as a goal.
